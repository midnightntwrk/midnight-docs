title: Blockchain space tokenization (BST)
description: Learn how blockchain space tokenization (BST) resolves the trade-off between low transaction fees and fast processing times.
---

Blockchains have a physical reality: limited room in each block for transactions. When too many people want to use the network at once, demand spikes and congestion becomes a major issue. Historically, users had to make a choice, either pay expensive fees, or accept long, uncertain wait times.

Peer-reviewed research by Input Output Research (IOR) proposes a fundamental shift in managing this scarce resource: **block space**. By introducing a new concept called blockchain space tokenization (BST), the network can tokenize transaction capacity, allowing users to pay in advance to secure the ability to post transactions regularly. 

### The congestion dilemma

Currently, there are two ways blockchains can handle congestion, but both have distinct structural flaws:

* **Auction and similar models (Bitcoin and Ethereum):** Users bid against each other and pay for faster transaction processing. **Fees are unpredictable**; a user might pay $1 one minute and $10 just a few minutes later.
* **Fixed-fee model (Cardano's current state):** Each transaction costs a predictable, flat rate. However, **delays become unpredictable**. If an attacker floods the network with thousands of transactions, legitimate transactions get stuck at the back of a massive line in a first-in, first-out (FIFO) approach.

This BST research solves a seemingly paradoxical problem. Before this paper, the industry consensus was that you had to choose between low fees or fast processing, and couldn't have both. BST breaks that trade-off.

### The solution: a subscription for block space

The research proposes moving from a pay-as-you-go model to a **subscription/reservation model** where the blockchain **tokenizes part of its future capacity** rather than paying when a transaction is sent.

Essentially, the right to put a transaction into a block is turned into a distinct digital token. Users or applications can buy these tokens in advance, granting them the right to post a certain number of transactions regularly over a period of time and ensuring that they have a **guaranteed slot**. Users who hold blockchain space tokens are mathematically guaranteed to have their transactions included within a very short, predictable window.

### Advantages of block space tokenization

There are many advantages to this novel approach of using blockchain space tokens, such as:

* **Enterprises:** Can budget exactly how much their blockchain usage will cost for the entire year and know that their supply chain data will never be delayed. 
* **DeFi:** Oracles (which provide price data) need to update every few seconds. BST ensures their updates always land on time, preventing market crashes caused by stale data.
* **Predictability:** Users know that transactions are processed in a predictable manner by implementing asymptotically optimal delays.
* **Layer 2 protocols:** Networks like Lightning set strict time bounds for participants to respond to and challenge network states. BST provides the predictable transaction delays necessary to prevent the severe security repercussions that occur when users miss these critical windows. 

### The trade off: challenges of block space tokenization

While the BST system mathematically guarantees a maximum delay for transactions, the "worst-case scenario" could still test a user's patience. Because priority is based partly on how much token value you hold, a user with a very small token balance might still face a significantly long maximum wait time during periods of extreme, adversarial network congestion.

### From the blackboard to reality

BST turns blockchain capacity into a stable, predictable, tokenized commodity that reduces delays and eliminates congestion. The practical impact of these concepts is already being explored; for instance, the Midnight blockchain incorporates several core ideas from this BST research. 

By leveraging these tokenization principles, Midnight is better equipped to manage its network resources and support its goal of offering rational privacy – ensuring that confidential business logic remains fluid, responsive, and affordable without compromising on cost predictability.
